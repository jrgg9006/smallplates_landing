# Brand Idea

*The idea that drives every decision. If you only remember one thing on this page, remember the north star.*

---

## THE IDEA

**The people who matter to you shouldn't disappear from your life when the wedding ends.**

A wedding is a single day. The guests arrive, they leave, they scatter. Small Plates exists so they don't.

---

## THE NORTH STAR

> **"Does this keep people from disappearing?"**

Every decision — product, copy, design, partnerships, pricing — passes through this question.

- If yes → proceed.
- If no → reconsider.
- If unclear → it probably doesn't. Cut it.

---

## PRESENCE, NOT MEMORY

The entire category confuses these. We don't.

| | Tense | What it says |
|---|---|---|
| Memory | Past | "They were there" |
| Keepsake | Past | "This reminds me of them" |
| **Presence** | **Present** | **"They are here"** |

Photo albums archive people. Small Plates keeps them active. Passive vs active presence is the only category distinction that matters.

---

## THE FOUR ARTICULATIONS

The idea stays the same. The compression changes by context.

| Level | Version | Use for |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategic** | *"The people who matter to you shouldn't disappear when the wedding ends."* | Investor decks, strategy docs, team alignment |
| **Shorthand** | *Vivid Presence* | Internal shorthand, creative briefs |
| **Poetic** | *Still at the table* | Tagline, packaging, brand line |
| **Functional** | *Recipes from the people who love you* | Ads, landing, acquisition |

These are not four ideas. They are one idea at four zoom levels.

---

## THE ORGANIZER LAYER

The idea has two faces. Who receives and who makes possible.

**To the couple:** *"The people who matter to you shouldn't disappear when the wedding ends."*

**To the organizer:** *"You made sure they stayed."*

The couple receives the presence. The organizer creates the conditions for it. Without her the people disappear like they always do. Every piece of communication to the organizer carries this second line, not the first.
