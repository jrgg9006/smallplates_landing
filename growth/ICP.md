# ICP — The Organizer
Last updated: April 2026

## Who She Is

She's not the bride. She's the person who makes things happen 
for the bride. The bridesmaid, the sister, the best friend. 
She's been at every major life moment and this is her way 
of making this one count.

Age: 26–38
Language: Bilingual or Spanish-dominant (MX market), 
          English-dominant (US market)

Markets: US, Mexico, European Union
Printing: Mixam (US/EU), El 78 (Mexico — artisanal hand-sewn)
Currency: USD primary

---

## Her Role in the Wedding

She's the organizer of the gift, not the recipient.
She found Small Plates, she pitched it to the group, 
she managed the guest list, she followed up on submissions.
She did the work because she wanted the bride to feel seen.

Primary organizer: bridesmaid, best friend, sister
Secondary: mother of the bride or groom — often involved 
but rarely the sole decision-maker. Always loops in 
the inner circle.

---

## Her Core Motivation

She doesn't want to give a gift that ends up in a closet.
She's seen that happen too many times — the crystal bowl, 
the picture frame, the generic registry item.

She wants something that means something. But she's also 
terrified of landing in "cheesy wedding gift" territory.
She needs it to be cool AND emotional. Not one or the other.

---

## What She Actually Says (Real Quotes, First 6 Weddings)

"Es un regalo padrísimo."
"Muy original, no lo había visto."
"Es muy cool."
"Es meaningful y muy bonito."

Read between the lines:
- "No lo había visto" = differentiation is the #1 trigger
- "Cool" + "meaningful" = she needed both to justify the purchase
- "Padrísimo" = emotional release after fear of being cheesy

---

## Her Fears

- "What if it looks cheap?"
- "What if guests don't submit?"
- "What if I'm the only one who thinks this is a good idea?"
- "What if it arrives late?"

---

## Her Triggers to Buy

- Sees it recommended by someone she trusts (friend, 
  Instagram account she respects, editorial mention)
- The product looks elevated, not crafty
- It feels doable — she can manage it without chasing 
  people for weeks
- There's a clear deadline and the book arrives on time
- Price feels justified for a group gift 
  ($169 split 5 ways = $34/person)

---

## Her Relationship With the Product

She's the buyer. But she also becomes emotionally attached 
to the process — collecting recipes, seeing the final book.
The showcase email (her recipe page) turns her into an 
advocate before the book even arrives.

This is the moment she goes from customer to referrer.

---

## Her Next Move (The Viral Loop)

She attends another wedding 6–18 months later.
Or her own engagement happens.
Or her sister gets engaged.

If the showcase email hit right, she remembers Small Plates 
not as "that gift I organized" but as "that thing I want 
for my own wedding."
