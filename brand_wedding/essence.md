# Small Plates & Co. — Essence

*This is who we are, in 2 minutes of reading. Every other doc in this system is a tool. This is the story.*

---

## What we believe

Everyone leaves.

That's the thing nobody in the wedding industry will tell you. They'll sell you the day — the dress, the flowers, the photographer, the seating chart, the music. They'll sell you the memory of the day. They won't tell you what happens after: the people who flew in fly home, the group chat goes quiet, the thank-you notes stop coming, and eighty people who were all in one room on one night are suddenly in eighty different cities, each with their own lives.

The industry is good at the day. The industry is silent about the afterward.

## Why we exist

The people who matter to you shouldn't disappear when the wedding ends.

Some of them will — that's just distance, life, time. But the ones who really matter don't have to. Not if we make something that keeps them in the room with you. Not in a photograph on a shelf, but in your hands, in your kitchen, in what you're making for dinner on a random Wednesday in March — a dinner you haven't cooked yet, with the people already at the table.

That's what Small Plates is for.

## What we make

A cookbook, written by every guest who showed up.

Each guest sends a recipe and a note. We collect them, design them, print them, and bind them into a hardcover book that gets gifted to the couple. Each recipe is a person. Each meal is a reunion. Each page is someone who was there, still there.

The book is the proof. The presence is the product. The book doesn't perform — it sits open on the counter, watching, while the eighty people behind it come to life in the kitchen.

## Who we're for

Small Plates exists because of one specific person: the organizer. The MOH, the sister, the mom, the best friend who got drafted into making something meaningful happen. She's the one who opens the group chat, sends the reminders, follows up with the guest who hasn't responded.

She's not overwhelmed — she's under-resourced. We give her a system that does the chasing, so she gets the glory without the grief. The bride receives the gift. The organizer receives the credit for the idea. Both feel seen.

## Why now

This is the first generation of couples who grew up watching the internet promise them belonging and deliver isolation. They don't want bigger weddings. They want closer ones. They want the people to stay.

We didn't build Small Plates for a trend. We built it because everyone leaves, and it didn't have to be that way. The trend found us.

## What we stand against

One-day thinking.

Gifts that disappear into cabinets. Wedding industries that vanish the moment the playlist ends. Memories frozen in photo albums nobody opens. Registries full of things nobody asked for. The whole economy of the beautiful moment, sold, performed, and abandoned.

Small Plates is the opposite of all of that. Not the moment — the afterward. Not the memory — the presence. Not archived — active.

## What "Small Plates & Co." means

The "& Co." isn't a corporate suffix.

It means the company of people. The eighty people in the book. The organizer who made it happen. The couple who receives it. All of us, at the same table, still.

That's the whole idea. Everything else is execution.
