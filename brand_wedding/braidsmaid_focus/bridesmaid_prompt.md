Recommendations for Deep Brand Positioning
To "fully speak" to them at a deep level, consider these pivots:

Pivot from "Gift" to "Tool for Connection": Frame the small plates not just as dinnerware, but as the centerpiece for the "intentional pausing" required during the stressful 12-month planning timeline.

Solve the "Sizing Anxiety": While plates don't have human sizes, bridesmaids themselves suffer from intense body surveillance and "sizing anxiety". Your branding should use "real-woman" imagery in its lifestyle photography to provide psychological relief and foster a sense of belonging.

Address the "Weeklong Wedding" Burnout: Bridesmaids often resent the "cumulative financial burden" of endless events. Position Small Plates and Company as the "One-and-Done" high-impact gift that replaces five smaller, "tacky" trinkets.

Adopt a "COO-to-COO" Tone: Use marketing copy that is "Respectful. Adult. No pressure". Instead of flowery language, use pragmatic, supportive, and even humorous tones that acknowledge the "manual labor" the bridesmaid is performing.
