# Characters — The Wedding Cast

Small Plates is not a product.
It’s a story with people inside.

These are not “personas”.
They are roles in an emotional system.

Every decision we make should work for at least one of them.
Great decisions work for more than one at once.

---

## The Bride — The Center

She is not the buyer most of the time.
She is the reason.

She lives inside:
- anticipation
- pressure
- expectation
- emotion
- nostalgia
- exhaustion
- hope

Her fear is not:
“Is this pretty?”
It’s:
“Will this mean something when it’s over?”

What she really wants:
- to feel seen
- to feel held
- to feel remembered
- to feel that the people around her matter as much as the day itself

Small Plates gives her:
not another detail,
but a piece of her people.

When she receives it, she should feel:
“They didn’t just celebrate me.
They know me.”

---

## The Organizer — The General

She is the engine. The one who actually makes things happen.

She carries:
- the logistics nobody sees
- the emotional weight of getting it right
- the pressure of representing the group
- the exhaustion of being the responsible one
- love — deep, fierce, unspoken

Her three fears:
- **Ineptitude:** “What if I can’t pull this off?”
- **Subjugation:** “What if I do all the work and someone else gets the credit — or worse, the bride never knows?”
- **Depletion:** “What if this drains me when I’m already running on fumes?”

Her secret question:
“Can I pull this off without losing my mind — and still make her cry?”

She doesn’t want:
another project to manage.
Another group chat to chase.
Another thing that depends entirely on her.

She wants:
glory without grief.
Proof she’s the friend who gets it right.
A done-for-you system that makes her look like a genius.

What Small Plates gives her:
- automated reminders (she doesn’t chase anyone)
- a dashboard that shows progress (she stays informed without doing the work)
- professional design (she doesn’t need taste or design skills)
- emotional credit (when the bride cries, she’s the one who made it happen)

When she gives it, she should feel:
“I didn’t just give her a gift.
I gave her all of her people — and I didn’t lose my mind doing it.”

---

## The Mother — The Guardian

She protects time.

She feels:
the wedding is not the point —
the life after it is.

She wants:
something that outlives the flowers.

Small Plates tells her:
“Her home will be filled with love.”

When she gives it, she should feel:
“This is not just for her wedding.
This is for her life.”

---

## The Planner — The Amplifier

She doesn’t buy.
She decides what matters.

She is allergic to:
- gimmicks
- cheap emotion
- obvious trends

She looks for:
- emotional weight
- aesthetic intelligence
- timelessness

Small Plates earns her when it feels:
- intentional
- dignified
- emotional without being loud

When she recommends it, it should feel:
“This elevates the wedding.”

---

## The Guests — The Contributors

They don’t buy.

They give:
a recipe
a memory
a part of themselves.

For them, Small Plates is:
a way to be present
before the wedding happens.

When they participate, they should feel:
“Now I’m part of something.”

---

## The Groom — The Witness

Not the focus.
But deeply aware.

He wants:
her to feel seen and held.

When Small Plates enters the picture, it should feel:
“This is something she’ll keep.”

---

## The System View

Organizer = catalyst and buyer
Bride = emotional center and taste gatekeeper
Mother = permanence
Planner = credibility
Guests = meaning
Groom = witness

Small Plates only works if:
two people feel chosen —
the bride as the center of the book,
and the organizer as the hero who made it happen.

The bride receives the gift.
The organizer receives the glory.
Both feel seen. Both feel held.
