# The Forgettable Gift

Not cheap.
Not wrong.
Not ugly.

Just... nothing.

Another blender.
Another cash fund.
Another thing that says "I was there"
without saying who you are.

The wedding ends.
The guests go home.
And the gift they left behind
doesn't carry a single trace of them.

No name.
No voice.
No presence.

Just an object.

---

# The Real Problem

The forgettable gift isn't a taste problem.
It's not even a budget problem.

It's a disappearance problem.

When a gift has no person inside it —
the person disappears.

The blender doesn't remember your best friend's laugh.
The cash fund doesn't carry your grandmother's handwriting.
The registry item doesn't know who gave it.

And three years into a marriage,
when the couple opens a cabinet
and sees that thing they got at the wedding —

they can't tell you who it's from.

That's not a bad gift.
That's a person who is no longer there.
