# Small Plates: Brand Idea

## THE IDEA

**People you love shouldn't disappear from your life.**

The forces of modern life (distance, time, technology, attention) work against this every day. We work for it.

---

## THE ENEMY

**The Forgettable.**

Most things made today are designed to be seen once and discarded. Gifts that go in a drawer. Posts that vanish in a swipe. Connection that performs itself and then disappears.

The more digital life gets, the more forgettable it gets.

---

## WHY WE EXIST

We exist to connect people through food.

To make the moments where someone feels loved by the people they love most. To bring people closer in the kitchen. To let a group discover each other again, one recipe at a time.

The book is how we do it.

---

## WHY NOW

Because people are looking for new ways to connect. And because the world is filling up with fake things made fast.

We go the other way: real things that last, that you can touch, built around the people who matter most in your life.

---

## WHAT WE MAKE

A collaborative recipe book from the people who love someone: for a wedding, a retirement, a graduation, a new baby, a birthday that matters. The organizer starts it free. Everyone contributes a recipe and a note through a link, from wherever they are. We clean, design, print, and bind. Payment happens once, when the book goes to print. It lives in the kitchen.

The book is the proof. Presence is the product.

This is the first thing we make. It won't be the last.

---

## WHY PHYSICAL

The obvious alternative is digital: a shared album, a website, a video montage. Cheaper. Easier. More shareable.

But a physical object made by a whole group carries a signal no individual purchase can replicate: *this required coordination.* Someone organized it. Everyone contributed. The weight of it, literal and figurative, is the proof. You can hold it. You can open it in front of people and watch them look for their own name. It passes from hand to hand. It gets stained and stays on the counter. Digital things don't do this.

The value is not the recipes. The value is the artifact of everyone taking part, made holdable and permanent.

This is also why price is a feature, not a problem. A cheap book would undermine the signal. Starting is free; the book is not. The price says: these people were worth it.

---

## WHAT "SMALL PLATES & CO." MEANS

The "& Co." isn't a corporate suffix.

It means *the company of people.* Whoever's in your life that matters: still here, still at the table.

That's the whole idea.

---

## WHO WE SERVE

**The organizer.** The one who opens the group chat and makes it happen. Full psychology in `characters.md`.

---

## HOW TO EXPLAIN IT

*Five sentences. For press, investors, cold intros, anyone who asks.*

Small Plates & Co. makes collaborative recipe books: one hardcover, written by the people who love someone. The organizer starts it free and shares a link; people send a recipe and a note. We design, print, and deliver the book with every contributor in it; payment happens once, when it goes to print. It lives in the kitchen, gets opened on a Tuesday, gets stained. Most gifts go in a drawer within a year; this one stays on the counter.

---

## WHAT WE PROMISE

We get people together through food, and we bring them closer to you.

Not in a feed. In your kitchen, in their handwriting, at your table.

---

## HOW WE POSITION

A gift that brings people together through the one thing everyone has: food.

What makes it different:

- Made by the whole group, not bought by one person.
- Real and physical. Printed, bound, built to be used, not stored.
- Impossible to buy anywhere else. The recipes and notes only exist in that group.
- Easy. One link, and we do the rest: clean, design, print.
