# Small Plates — Foundation

## OUR BELIEF

**The wedding industry sells a day. We build for every day after.**

The day ends. The guests leave. The objects fade. The 80 people who were there scatter — some stay close, most don't. Small Plates exists because the objects you buy for a wedding should work for the life after the wedding, not for the day of the wedding.

We are not fighting a product. We are fighting the idea that a wedding is a day.

---

## OUR ENEMY

**Everyone leaves.**

That's the thing nobody in the wedding industry will tell you. The industry sells the day. It is silent about the afterward — the part where eighty people scatter and nobody tells you how to keep the ones that matter.

Small Plates is for the afterward.

**Vocabulary this generates** (use in copy where natural):
*the leaving · the afterward · who stays · the scattering · after everyone leaves · before they scatter · the silent afterward · one-day thinking · the forgettable gift*

---

## WHO WE SERVE

**The organizer.** The MOH, the sister, the mom, the best friend who got drafted into orchestrating something meaningful. She is 25–38. She carries emotional labor for someone she loves, and pays for part of it. She is terrified of showing up with a bath set. She bridges late millennial and early Gen Z. The brand was built for the millennial wedding but belongs to the Gen Z one.

**She isn't buying a book. She's buying the photo her friend will send her four months later** — the book open on the counter, flour dusting the pages, with a caption that says *"dude. Best idea anyone had."* That photo is the purchase motivation.

---

## WHAT WE PROMISE

The wedding is not over. The people who showed up are still here — in their handwriting, their recipes, their stories. The kitchen becomes where the guest list lives on.

**We don't promise memories. We promise presence.**

Active, not archived. Photo albums are passive — you have to go looking. Small Plates is active — the people appear, uninvited, while you cook.

---

## ALWAYS / NEVER

**Always:** show the book in use (stains, open, real kitchens) · talk about what comes after, not the day itself · say the quiet part · credit the guest contributors as co-authors · write like the smartest friend at the table.

**Never:** photograph the book as a styled keepsake · use the words *cherish, treasure, memories, special, unique, perfect, amazing, curated, journey, loved ones, celebrate* · lead with tears · show faces · use stock wedding imagery · call the wedding "the most important day."

## THE FILTER

When in doubt, one question:

> **"Does this keep people from disappearing?"**

If yes → ship it. If no → reconsider. If unclear → it probably doesn't. Cut it.

For taste and tone calls, pass it through **Margot** (see `margot.md`).
For the mental picture of where the brand lives, picture **her Tuesday table** — open book, stained pages, real kitchen. That's not a filter. That's the place we're building for.
