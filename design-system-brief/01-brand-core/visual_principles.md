# Visual Principles

*How Small Plates looks in practice. The operational visual language of the brand.*

*This document translates the strategic positioning in `category-rules.md` into concrete principles for photography, cinematography, and visual direction. Derived from interview with Olivia Dean's Director of Photography, April 2026, adapted to Small Plates — a brand whose subject is a collective object, not an individual person.*

---

## The reconnaissance test

If we cover the logo on any Small Plates content, the piece must still be recognizable as Small Plates. Not through a single gimmick — through consistent application of the principles below.

This test is the purpose of this document. Every principle listed exists because it contributes to making Small Plates visually unmistakable across multiple pieces produced by multiple people over time.

---

## PRINCIPLE 1 — ANCHOR ATMOSPHERE FIRST

Before paleta. Before camera. Before lenses. Decide **how the atmosphere feels.**

Small Plates' atmosphere is defined:

- Soft shadows, not hard contrast
- Warm window-like light, not studio softbox
- Rich textures (paper, fabric, skin, food, wood)
- Intimacy, calm
- Unhurried pace

This is not "status" (symmetric wide-angle). Not "efficiency" (flat clinical light). Not "drama" (high contrast saturated). Small Plates lives in **intimacy + warmth + texture.** Any decision that pulls away from this atmosphere should be questioned.

---

## PRINCIPLE 2 — CONSISTENT LANGUAGE, VARIABLE THEME

- **Consistent (the language):** colorimetry (warm earth tones, gold skin tones, matte finish), shadow treatment, grain quality, camera movement rhythm, typography
- **Variable (the theme):** space, scale, scene, situation

A frame from any Small Plates piece should be identifiable as Small Plates even if the situation changes completely (kitchen vs. restaurant vs. dining table vs. domestic interior). Consistency is the *how*. Variation is the *what*.

---

## PRINCIPLE 3 — THE ENAMORED CAMERA

The camera is proactive, not reactive.

- **Lenses:** 35mm or 50mm, not teleobjetivos (documentary distance)
- **Proximity:** physical closeness, inside the personal space of the subject
- **Movement:** slow drift, nearly imperceptible. Not stillness, not handheld kinetic
- **Rhythm:** pausado, like deep breathing
- **Ethos:** the camera "caresses the edges of the frame" — it doesn't just observe, it regards

For Small Plates, the subject of the enamored camera is the book, not a person. **We treat the book as we would treat Olivia's skin.** Close to the fibers, the stitching, the printed typography. Lateral light revealing texture. Reverent but not sacred.

---

## PRINCIPLE 4 — FOREGROUND THAT BUILDS, NEVER CONTAMINATES

Foreground elements are never casual.

- **Choose:** objects that share the palette and add texture but have no dominant recognizable form. A petal. The edge of a curtain. The blur of a hand entering frame. A glass half-drunk.
- **Avoid:** objects with strong recognizable forms that compete for attention with the subject.

**The rule:** the foreground must frame, not cover. Must build depth, not narrate a second story.

For Small Plates specifically, foreground elements sugerir las 80 personas invisibles: a wine glass half-consumed, a hand barely entering the frame, the wear on a wooden table, a napkin with sauce, a fork resting. Evidence of presence.

---

## PRINCIPLE 5 — SUGGESTIVE LIGHT, NOT STUDIO LIGHT

Light must look like it's entering through a window of a real home, not from a softbox in a studio. The grano de celuloide (simulated or real) removes clinical digital sharpness. Skin tones render warm golden with soft shadows. Diffusion filters (Pro-Mist, Black Glimmerglass) make highlights bloom slightly.

**Translation to Small Plates:** when the book is photographed, lateral window light reveals the texture of paper, the gold of the title, the stitching of the binding. When humans appear around the book, their skin renders warm not clinical. No harsh flash. No overhead fluorescent.

---

## PRINCIPLE 6 — THE SUBJECT IS INVISIBLE; PHOTOGRAPH THE EVIDENCE

Olivia Dean is visible. Our subject — the 80 people — is invisible.

We don't fight this. We photograph **evidence**:

- An empty chair someone just left
- Two half-eaten plates
- A cup with a lipstick mark
- A notebook open with a handwritten recipe
- The wear of use on an object

The presence is suggested by the things people leave behind, not by their faces. This principle extends A2.1 (no couple protagonism) to no human protagonism at all. Human traces, yes. Human faces as subjects, no.

---

## PRINCIPLE 7 — PHOTOGRAPH "THE WAIT FOR THE BANQUET," NOT THE BOOK

This is the most important mental shift in the DP's guidance.

**Don't photograph the book. Photograph anticipation.**

The book appears in frames where something is about to happen:
- Ingredients being prepared
- A table being set
- A pot simmering
- A glass being poured
- A kitchen in mid-action

**The book is the silent witness, not the protagonist.** The camera loves the book, but the frame shows the life around it. This is how we photograph "collective presence" without photographing the 80 people directly.

---

## PRINCIPLE 8 — MINI-FILMS WITH SITUATIONS, NOT STYLED PRODUCT SHOTS

Every piece of content is a situation with a world around it. Never a naked flat-lay. Never a styled shelf. Never a pristine hero shot without context.

- Real movement (not choreographed)
- Natural gestures
- Mise-en-scène that tells a micro-story
- The book present but integrated, not isolated

---

## PRINCIPLE 9 — VAGUE TEMPORAL REFERENCE

Olivia Dean evokes the 70s/80s without cosplaying them. Small Plates should evoke something too, but it's not the same era.

**Working hypothesis** (to validate in production): Small Plates evokes **the atemporal domestic kitchen** — not modern, not nostalgic, not retro. The kitchen where food is made by hand. The table where more than one generation has eaten. References could include: Martha Stewart Living in its early editorial days, Julia Child kitchens (not Julia herself), family cookbooks passed down with stains and margins, Italian/Mexican/Middle Eastern home cooking photographed with reverence.

**To confirm with Ana Karen and production tests before canonizing.**

---

## PRINCIPLE 10 — IMPERFECCIÓN HONESTA (HONEST IMPERFECTION)

The DP's closing advice is a direct guideline for the category:

> *"La industria de las bodas está saturada de imágenes perfectas, excesivamente iluminadas y blancas. Para ser reconocible, no temas a las sombras ni al grano."*

Operational rules:

- Don't fear shadows
- Don't fear grain
- Don't fear the unstyled moment
- Don't fear the object slightly worn
- Reject the wedding industry "light & airy" default

The aesthetic goal is a day that stays, not a day that shines.

## CANONIZED VOCABULARY — NEW TERMS FOR INTERNAL USE

Added to the Small Plates internal lexicon:

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Anchor atmosphere** | The first visual decision: how does this world feel? |
| **Rich minimalism** | Few elements, rich texture. Not empty. Not crowded. |
| **Modern nostalgia** | A vivid memory, not an archived one |
| **Enamored camera** | Proactive intimate camera, 35–50mm, slow drift |
| **Build vs. contaminate** | Rule for choosing foreground elements |
| **Honest imperfection** | The opposite of wedding industry "light & airy" |
| **The silent witness** | The book in a scene: present but not protagonist |
| **The wait for the banquet** | What we photograph instead of the book itself |
| **The texture of the feeling** | What we sell (not the product, the feeling's texture) |
| **Nostalgia of a dinner not yet happened** | The afterward rendered visually |

---

## TWO LINES FOR THE CANON

From the DP interview, two lines that earn their place in Small Plates' permanent vocabulary:

> *"The book is the silent witness of the connection of those 80 people."*

> *"People don't buy a cookbook. They buy the nostalgia of a dinner that hasn't happened yet. Sell the texture of that feeling."*

Both go into the brand vocabulary as guiding articulations. They extend and sharpen what `essence.md` already says, from the angle of image rather than narrative.

## WHAT THIS DOC DOES NOT RESOLVE

Explicit flags for future work. These are the operational outputs of this system that still need founder-led execution.

1. **The cover of the book.** Founder has explicitly identified current cover as "inside the rules of weddings and cookbooks" and wants it to be distinctive. Founder-led work session pending. Principles above feed into that work but don't resolve it.
2. **The hero shot of the product.** The single recurring composition that becomes Small Plates' visual signature. Still in discovery. Principles 3, 6, 7 point the direction.
3. **The world kit.** Three recurring visual framings that, repeated, become unmistakable. Still in discovery.
4. **The aesthetic reference phrase.** The short phrase (e.g., "X with the dry humor of Y") that synthesizes the visual world in under 10 words. Still in discovery. Principle 9 is a working hypothesis, not a commitment.
5. **Production test of Principle 9** (vague temporal reference — atemporal domestic kitchen). To validate with Ana Karen through actual shoots before canonizing.
