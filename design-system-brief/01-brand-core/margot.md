# Margot

*The filter. Not a persona. Not a customer. She is the brand's taste — the person every line of copy, every image, every decision has to pass through.*

*When in doubt, ask: **"Would Margot love it, ignore it, or find it cringe?"***
*If she'd love it: ship it.*
*If she'd ignore it: improve it.*
*If it'd make her cringe: kill it.*

---

## WHO SHE IS (ENOUGH TO OPERATE)

28. Brooklyn. Creative Director at an independent studio. Partnered, not married, not in a rush. Cooks actual food. Has strong taste she doesn't announce. She is the person every audience — bride, MOH, mom — sees themselves reflected in, from a different angle.

*Full character study lives in `/archive/margot-character-study.md` for anyone who wants depth.*

---

## THE TWO TESTS

**The Room Test.**
> Would Margot, glass of natural wine in hand at a dinner party that got real at 11pm, say this out loud?

If she'd roll her eyes → rewrite.
If she'd say it with a knowing smile → ship it.

**The Ojai Test.**
> Would the MOH at a 40-person wedding in someone's backyard in Ojai, who found Small Plates at 2am on TikTok, feel like this brand was made for her — or for her mom?

If it feels made for her mom → rewrite.
If it feels like the mom will discover it *through* her and love it → ship it.

(The brand travels daughter to mother. Never the other way.)

## HOW SHE SOUNDS

**When she likes something:** *"This is so good."* / *"Okay wait. This is actually — yeah."* / *"Very me."* / *"Trust me on this one."*

**When she doesn't:** *"It's… fine."* / *"It's a lot."* / *"They're working really hard at this."* / *"Honestly? Skip it."*

**When she talks about weddings:** *"I ugly cried and I'm not apologizing for it."* / *"I love them and it was a lot of money for one day."* / About Small Plates: *"Why didn't this exist before."* (Not a question.)

---

## WORDS SHE WOULD NEVER SAY

*Cherish. Treasure. Journey. Amazing. Curated. Perfect. So blessed. Loved ones. Yummy. Memories. Celebrate. Special. Unique.*

And: *"I'm obsessed"* for everything. *"Literally dying"* as a reflex. Exclamation points more than once per piece.

If Margot wouldn't say it, Small Plates doesn't.

---

## WHAT SHE ALWAYS DOES

- Leads with the specific, not the general
- Trusts the audience to feel without being told to feel
- Leaves more white space than feels comfortable
- Cuts the last sentence (that's where the explanation hides)
- Chooses the photograph where something is slightly off over the one that's perfect
- Says the true thing that makes people slightly uncomfortable

## WHAT SHE WOULD NEVER DO

- Use a stock photo of a couple with their mouths open in joy
- Write a tagline that sounds like a tagline
- Explain why something is emotional instead of making it emotional
- Show the product as the most important thing in the room
- Design something that looks like it came from The Knot

---

## IF SHE HAD TO DEFEND THE BRAND IN ONE LINE

*"The wedding industry sells a day. We build for every day after."*

That's her. That's the brand. Same sentence.
