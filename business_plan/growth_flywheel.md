## 1. Introduction

### What is a Growth Flywheel?

A growth flywheel is a self-reinforcing cycle where each action feeds the next, creating compounding momentum over time. Unlike linear funnels that require constant input, a flywheel gains energy with each rotationâ€”the more it spins, the faster it goes.

**Why it matters for Small Plates:**

Every book we sell doesn't just generate revenueâ€”it generates future customers. Each book reaches 50-100 wedding guests who experience our brand firsthand. Some of those guests will get married. Some will organize gifts for friends. The product IS the marketing.

### Our Flywheel Philosophy

> **"The product sells the next product."**

Traditional DTC brands spend heavily on acquisition because their product disappears after purchase. A candle burns. A shirt wears out. But a Small Plates book:

- Lives in the couple's kitchen for decades
- Gets shown to every visitor
- Contains the names of 50-100 people who contributed
- Creates emotional moments every time it's opened

This is our unfair advantage. We don't just acquire customersâ€”we acquire evangelists.

## 3. Core Flywheel

The core flywheel represents the fundamental engine of Small Plates' growth. Every element is essentialâ€”if any step breaks, the flywheel stops.

### Node 1: Organizer Buys

**What happens:**
A bridesmaid, maid of honor, sister, or close friend purchases a Small Plates book as a wedding gift.

**Why it matters:**
This is the entry point. Without a purchase, nothing else happens. The organizer is our primary customerâ€”she holds the credit card and manages the entire process.

**Key metrics:**
- Conversion rate from visitor to purchase
- Average order value (AOV)
- Cost per acquisition (CPA)

**What influences this:**
- Marketing (paid + organic)
- Word of mouth from previous customers
- Referrals from guests who contributed to other books
- Wedding planner recommendations
- Brand awareness

---

### Node 2: Guests Contribute

**What happens:**
The organizer invites 50-150 wedding guests to submit recipes. Guests receive an email invitation, click a link, and submit their recipe + personal message in approximately 5 minutes.

**Why it matters:**
Guest participation determines book quality. A book with 50+ recipes feels abundant and meaningful. A book with 15 recipes feels sparse. High participation = happy bride = positive word of mouth.

**Key metrics:**
- Invitation-to-contribution rate
- Average recipes per book
- Time from invitation to submission
- Drop-off points in submission flow

**What influences this:**
- Quality of invitation email (copy, design, clarity)
- Simplicity of submission process (friction reduction)
- Reminder email effectiveness
- Social proof ("23 people have already submitted")
- Organizer's personal follow-up with guests

---

### Node 3: Guests Know Small Plates

**What happens:**
Every guest who receives an invitationâ€”whether they submit or notâ€”now knows Small Plates exists. Those who contribute have a deeper, positive brand experience.

**Why it matters:**
This is **free brand exposure at scale**. Each book sold introduces 50-150 people to Small Plates. No ad spend required.

**Key metrics:**
- Total guests invited per book
- Brand recall among contributors (survey)
- Email open rates (proxy for awareness)

**What influences this:**
- Email design and branding consistency
- Quality of the contribution experience
- Confirmation messaging post-submission
- Overall brand impression (Margot Cole filter)

---

### Node 4: Book Delivered

**What happens:**
The completed book is printed, shipped, and delivered to the organizer, who presents it to the couple (usually at the wedding, bridal shower, or as a private gift).

**Why it matters:**
The physical product is the ultimate proof of value. When the bride opens it, the emotional reaction validates everything. This moment creates evangelists.

**Key metrics:**
- Delivery time (promise vs. actual)
- Product quality score
- Unboxing experience rating
- Bride reaction (NPS, testimonials)

**What influences this:**
- Print quality and consistency
- Packaging and presentation
- Delivery reliability
- Managing expectations throughout process

---

### Node 5: Book Lives in Kitchen

**What happens:**
Unlike most wedding gifts that end up in closets, Small Plates is designed to live in the couple's kitchen. It's used for actual cooking. It sits on the counter or cookbook shelf. It's visible.

**Why it matters:**
Every time someone visits the couple's home, they potentially see the book. Every dinner party. Every holiday. For years or decades. The book continues marketing itself long after the wedding.

**Key metrics:**
- Self-reported usage frequency
- Placement (kitchen vs. storage)
- Longevity (still visible after 1, 5, 10 years?)

**What influences this:**
- Recipe quality and usability
- Book durability
- Design aesthetic (something they're proud to display)
- Practical functionality (lies flat, wipeable, etc.)

---

### Node 6: Future Guest Buys

**What happens:**
A guest who contributed to someone else's book gets engaged, or their friend gets engaged. They remember Small Plates. They become a customer or recommend it to whoever is organizing the gift.

**Why it matters:**
This closes the loop. The flywheel becomes self-sustaining. Past guests become future customers without additional acquisition cost.

**Key metrics:**
- % of new customers who previously contributed
- Time from contribution to purchase (guest lifecycle)
- Referral rate from contributors

**What influences this:**
- Quality of original contribution experience
- Email nurturing post-contribution
- Ease of re-finding Small Plates (SEO, brand recall)
- Life stage timing (they need to know someone getting married)
