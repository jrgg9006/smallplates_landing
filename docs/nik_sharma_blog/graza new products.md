Graza is a fun case study…let’s dig in!

They launched in January 2022 with just Drizzle and Sizzle. That gave people a very easy way to understand the brand from day one. One bottle for finishing and one for cooking. Clean idea and an easy repeat purchase.

Then they let that duo do the heavy lifting for a while.

Over time, they expanded with a lot more intention than most brands do. In February 2025, Graza launched Frizzle, their high-heat cooking oil, and brought it out in more than one format, including a spray bottle.

In August 2025, they launched their first glass bottles for Drizzle and Sizzle.

Then in March 2026, they finally moved into condiments with Graza Mayo. 

That is roughly four years of growth without chucking random SKUs into the mix every time they got a little attention.

Yes, there were side moments along the way.

Graza did limited-edition or collab-style moves like chips and a hummus collab with Ithaca. But the core line expansion stayed pretty disciplined.

The big permanent moves came one at a time, with enough space for each
one to mean something. 



Why it
stood out?
They treated SKU expansion like brand building. Each launch had a job to do, and each added something the customer could understand easily.

There’s a good lesson in that.

A lot of brands get a bit of traction, then suddenly want to launch five new things because the team is excited, the factory has options, or someone thinks “more products” means “more growth”. (spoiler, it doesn’t!)

Usually it just makes the brand harder to understand.

Graza gave people time to get attached to the first thing. Then they built out from there in a way that still felt connected to the original idea….

That is the bit worth stealing.

Know what your audience is already coming to you for. Understand where the next product naturally fits. Test as you grow. Add SKUs when the market is ready for them and when the brand has earned the right to stretch.

Because good expansion makes the brand feel bigger.

Messy expansion just makes the shelf feel crowded.
