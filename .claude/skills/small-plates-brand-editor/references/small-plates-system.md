# Small Plates — Canonical System

---

## 1. The company

Small Plates & Co. is a DTC wedding gift product. Wedding guests submit recipes and personal notes that are compiled into a professionally designed, printed hardcover cookbook, gifted to the couple.

**The product is not a book. The product is presence.** The book is the proof.

Founders: Ricardo (CTO, operations, back-end, tech stack) and Ana Karen (public-facing, community, operations).

Structure: Wyoming LLC, manager-managed.

---

## 2. The core belief

**The wedding industry sells a day. We build for every day after.**

The day ends. The guests leave. The objects fade. The 80 people who were there scatter — some stay close, most don't. Small Plates exists because the objects you buy for a wedding should work for the life after the wedding, not for the day of the wedding.

We are not fighting a product. We are fighting the idea that a wedding is a day.

---

## 3. The enemy

**Everyone leaves.**

That's the thing nobody in the wedding industry will tell you. The industry sells the day. It is silent about the afterward — the part where eighty people scatter and nobody tells you how to keep the ones that matter.

Small Plates is for the afterward.

**Vocabulary this generates** (use in copy where natural):
*the leaving · the afterward · who stays · the scattering · after everyone leaves · before they scatter · the silent afterward · one-day thinking · the forgettable gift*

"One-day thinking" and "the forgettable gift" are retained as tactical variants subordinate to the primary enemy framing. They are specific expressions of "everyone leaves" — not parallel enemies. Updated April 2026 (founder-validated reframing from "the one-day problem").

---

## 4. The north star question

> **"Does this keep people from disappearing?"**

Every decision — product, copy, design, partnerships, pricing — passes through this question.

- Yes → proceed.
- No → reconsider.
- Unclear → it probably doesn't. Cut it.

---

## 5. Who we serve

**The organizer.** The MOH, the sister, the mom, the best friend who got drafted into orchestrating something meaningful. She is 25–38. She carries emotional labor for someone she loves, and pays for part of it. She is terrified of showing up with a bath set.

**She isn't buying a book. She's buying the photo her friend will send her four months later** — the book open on the counter, flour dusting the pages, with a caption that says *"dude. Best idea anyone had."*

That photo is the purchase motivation.

---

## 6. What we promise

The wedding is not over. The people who showed up are still here — in their handwriting, their recipes, their stories. The kitchen becomes where the guest list lives on.

**We don't promise memories. We promise presence.**

Active, not archived. Photo albums are passive — you have to go looking. Small Plates is active — the people appear, uninvited, while you cook.

## 8. Presence, not memory

The category confuses these. We don't.

| | Tense | What it says |
|---|---|---|
| Memory | Past | "They were there" |
| Keepsake | Past | "This reminds me of them" |
| **Presence** | **Present** | **"They are here"** |

Photo albums archive people. Small Plates keeps them active. This is the category distinction that matters.

## 9. Margot — the taste filter

Margot is the 28-year-old Brooklyn Creative Director who is the brand. She is not a persona. She is not a customer. She is the editorial filter every piece of copy passes through.

**The test:** *"Would Margot, glass of wine in hand at a real dinner party at 11pm, say this out loud?"* If she'd roll her eyes, rewrite. If she'd say it with a knowing smile, ship it.

**The secondary test (Gen Z alignment):** *"Would the MOH at a 40-person wedding in someone's backyard in Ojai, who found Small Plates at 2am on TikTok, feel like this was made for her — or for her mom?"* The brand must travel daughter-to-mother, never the other way.

**Margot would say:** *"This is so good." / "Okay wait, this is actually — yeah." / "Very me." / "Trust me on this one." / "I ugly cried and I'm not apologizing for it." / "Why didn't this exist before."* (Not a question.)

**Margot would not say:** *"It's… fine." / "It's a lot." / "They're working really hard at this." / "Honestly? Skip it."* (These are what she says about *bad* things. Useful for self-check.)

## 10. Always / Never (summary)

**Always:** show the book in use (stains, open, real kitchens) · talk about what comes after, not the day itself · say the quiet part · credit the guest contributors as co-authors · write like the smartest friend at the table.

**Never:** photograph the book as a styled keepsake · lead with tears · show faces · use stock wedding imagery · call the wedding "the most important day."

**Banned vocabulary** (absolute, no exceptions): *cherish, treasure, memories, special, unique, perfect, amazing, curated, journey, loved ones, celebrate. So blessed. Yummy. "I'm obsessed" as default. "Literally dying" as reflex.*

## 11. The five manifesto ruptures (A1)

The brand is defined as much by what it breaks as by what it builds. Cult brands have 3–5 manifest ruptures — the rules whose breaking *is* the brand. Small Plates has five, canonized in `category_rules.md` (April 2026). Every piece of work (copy, product, photography, design) should carry at least one of them legibly. If a piece carries none of the five, it's not Small Plates.

- **A1.1 — Reject the entire verbal and narrative playbook of the wedding industry.** Opposite framing: *"everyone leaves / what stays / the afterward."* Banned vocabulary enforced (see §10). Guests elevated as co-authors; the couple is not the sole protagonist.
- **A1.2 — The buyer is the organizer, not the couple.** Behaviorally defined (the proactive character), not demographically. Product-level segmentation stops at the organizer.
- **A1.3 — The gift is collective, not individual.** 80 guests co-construct a single object. The product is a collaborative artifact, not an accumulation of individual gifts.
- **A1.4 — Value activates in the afterward, even when the object is delivered during.** Built for the Wednesday, not for the wedding.
- **A1.5 — The only wedding gift that activates socially when opened.** The book passes hand to hand when unwrapped in front of guests. Structural to the design. Our viral coefficient.

A2 (recognized ruptures), A2.5 (intentional deviations), B (ignored), and C (respected deliberately) classifications live in `category_rules.md`. Consult before editing copy, photography briefs, or positioning documents.

## 12. The visual internal lexicon

Ten terms canonized April 2026 in `visual_principles.md`. Use these when discussing the brand internally; full definitions live in the source doc.

| Term | Use |
|---|---|
| **Anchor atmosphere** | The first visual decision: how does this world feel? |
| **Rich minimalism** | Few elements, rich texture. Not empty. Not crowded. |
| **Modern nostalgia** | A vivid memory, not an archived one. |
| **Enamored camera** | Proactive intimate camera, 35–50mm, slow drift. |
| **Build vs. contaminate** | Rule for choosing foreground elements. |
| **Honest imperfection** | The opposite of wedding industry "light & airy." |
| **The silent witness** | The book in a scene: present but not protagonist. |
| **The wait for the banquet** | What we photograph instead of the book itself. |
| **The texture of the feeling** | What we sell (not the product — the feeling's texture). |
| **Nostalgia of a dinner not yet happened** | The afterward rendered visually. |

Two canonized articulations from the DP interview (April 2026):

> *"The book is the silent witness of the connection of those 80 people."*
>
> *"People don't buy a cookbook. They buy the nostalgia of a dinner that hasn't happened yet. Sell the texture of that feeling."*

Both sharpen what `essence.md` says, from the angle of image rather than narrative.

## 13. File system (the canonical brand docs)

The system has **five canonical pillars** plus a **three-doc tactical toolkit**.

### The five pillars (strategic and narrative)

- **`foundation.md`** — the one-page operating system. What we do, who we serve, enemy, promise, always/never.
- **`brand_idea.md`** — the strategic core, the north star question, the four articulations.
- **`margot.md`** — the taste filter, how she sounds, the two tests.
- **`organizer.md`** — the audience operational profile. Her behavioral frame, three fears, secret question, trust model, objection handling, and copy toolkit.
- **`essence.md`** — the narrative declaration. The 2-minute read that tells the story behind the system. Founder-approved. Primary source for external-facing communications (press, site about, team onboarding).

### The tactical toolkit (three companion docs)

- **`visual-identity.md`** — the tactical visual toolkit. Canonical for color, typography, logo, book mechanics, and visual prohibitions. Pending: hero shot, world kit, aspirational references, aesthetic reference phrase (these will be resolved in a founder-led discovery process, not editorial compression).
- **`category_rules.md`** — the strategic positioning document. Maps where Small Plates stands against the wedding and cookbook industries. Canonizes the 5 A1 manifesto ruptures (see §11). Also defines A2 recognized ruptures, A2.5 intentional deviations, B ignored rules, and C respected-deliberately rules.
- **`visual_principles.md`** — the operational visual language. Ten principles (anchor atmosphere, enamored camera, the silent witness, the wait for the banquet, etc.) that translate strategic positioning into photography, cinematography, and visual direction. Source of the internal visual lexicon (see §12).

When the user asks you to edit brand docs, these eight are the system. Anything else is archive or work-in-progress. Other docs (`characters.md`, `tagline-system.md` residual) serve specific executional purposes and should be kept minimal.
